Wrong-Way Driver Detection — Beyond Ramps

A lightweight, sensor-free system that detects wrong-way vehicles using GPS bearing vs. OSM road geometry. No cameras. No ML model. Works anywhere with open map data.

HOW IT WORKS

Step 1 — Road geometry from OSM

We pull the real road network using OpenStreetMap via osmnx. Each road segment has a known allowed travel direction (bearing).

Step 2 — Vehicle bearing from GPS

From consecutive GPS points we compute the vehicle's heading using the haversine bearing formula.

Step 3 — Compare and flag

If a vehicle's bearing differs from the road's allowed direction by more than 120° for 3+ consecutive points, it is flagged as a wrong-way intruder.

FALSE POSITIVE HANDLING

Problem: Temporary road works or diversions may cause legitimate vehicles to appear wrong-way.

Mitigation: The 3-point persistence filter reduces one-off GPS noise. In production, OSM live data or a known-diversion feed would whitelist these segments temporarily.
Problem: U-turns near intersections can trigger a false flag.

Mitigation: Suppress alerts within 20m of an intersection node where U-turns are permitted.

WHY THIS MATTERS

  • Works on urban grids, not just freeway ramps
  • No expensive sensors — just GPS + open map data
  • Applicable to fleet safety, ride-hailing, micromobility
  • Alerts can be pushed to surrounding connected vehicles in real time
